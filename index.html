Where in the chain is food lost?

Food loss and waste occurs at every step of the supply chain — from the farm through to your kitchen bin. The causes differ sharply between high- and low-income countries.

In high-income countries, over 40% of waste happens at retail and consumer level. In low-income countries, most losses occur at production and handling stages due to infrastructure gaps and climate vulnerability.

Why it matters beyond your bin

When food is wasted, everything used to produce it — land, water, energy, labour — is wasted too. The consequences ripple across climate, economy, and society.

What can you do today?

Reducing food waste is one of the highest-impact climate actions available to individuals. Small daily habits compound into big results.

Reducing household food waste by just 50% could save the average Indian family ₹12,000–18,000 per year, while cutting the carbon equivalent of taking a car off the road for several weeks. Food waste reduction ranks in the top 10 climate solutions globally according to Project Drawdown.
